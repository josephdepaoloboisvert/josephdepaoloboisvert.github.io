Chemist, Founder, Deckhand —
One Person, Three Beats

BY THE EDITOR · CHICAGO · A LONG-RUNNING SELF-PROFILE

Joseph A. DePaolo-Boisvert is a doctoral candidate in chemistry at the Illinois Institute of Technology, where he studies the molecular dynamics of membrane proteins under Dr. David D. L. Minh. His thesis lives at the seam between statistical mechanics, physics based simulation, and machine learning.

Away from the lab he is the co-founder and chief technology officer of Biagon, Inc., a biotech he and a small team have been building since early 2024. Weekends and in peak summer season, he works the on deck of Spirit of Navy Pier — a third job originally taken by necessity but through shipboard maintainence, emergency response training, and hospitality work has instilled an operational calm the lab doesn't quite demand.

Originally from Southington, Connecticut, he obtained a double bachelor's degree in Physics and Chemistry from Boston University. Arriving in Chicago in the full hectic swing of COVID-19, he has been here ever since. Before the lab, the company, and the boat consumed the calendar, he played jazz piano well enough to miss it, and football through D3 and one season of semi-professional play.

This Dispatch collects the three beats in one place: recent work, essays and notes, and a printable cv. The masthead is a joke about taking oneself slightly too seriously; while the writing tries not to.

New issues appear when there is something worth reporting. Updates are irregular. Correspondence is welcomed.
