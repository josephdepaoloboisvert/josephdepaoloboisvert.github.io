How Many Variables Describe a Protein, Really?

Proteins can comprise thousands of atoms, yet are typically folded into a small number of three-dimensional conformations. The coordinate space is enormous; the space of behaviours, mercifully, is not. This essay — drawn from chapter three of my thesis — is about that gap, and what we get to do once we admit it exists.

“Biologically meaningful dynamics often reside on low-dimensional manifolds, even when embedded in an extremely high-dimensional coordinate space.”

The classical answer is dimensionality reduction: principal components, time-lagged components, diffusion maps. Each works, each has costs. What I want to argue is that the right number is not a property of the protein but of the question, and that an autoencoder — properly regularised — is one of the few tools that lets you ask both at once.

The rest of the piece is on the website proper. Skip ahead if you’ve heard the manifold pitch before; stay if you’d like the version with figures.

On the Causes of Seasickness

Seasickness is not an illness — it is a complaint filed by the brain when the inner ear, the eyes, and the body disagree about what the world is doing. The most provocative condition is slow vertical and rotary motion with no visual horizon: the inside of a cabin on a rocky lake michigan day, for instance. The vestibular system bears most of the responsibility; people with absent vestibular function do not get seasick, which is one of the more elegant natural experiments available to the field. Susceptibility varies by age, sex, and sleep deprivation — there is a hereditary component — and experienced sailors adapt over days to months of repeated exposure. Symptoms progress predictably: malaise and yawning before nausea, nausea before vomiting, sweating and pallor throughout. Non-pharmaceutical treatment reduces to one principle: minimize the conflict. Seek the horizon, stand where the boat moves least, let fresh air in, wait. Drugs help, but placebo accounts for ten to forty percent of apparent drug benefit — which is another way of saying that confidence and expectation are themselves a mechanism, and that acting like you know what you are doing is not entirely theatrical (in this regard).
